SIBO-Adaptive Recomposition Engine (SARE)
Precision Metabolism & Gut Integrity Management
🎯 Executive Summary
Most fitness applications rely on generic formulas that ignore the delicate balance between aggressive fat loss and digestive health. This project is a custom-built analytical tool designed for individuals managing SIBO (Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth) while pursuing structural body recomposition.

The engine replaces the "black box" approach of standard apps with a transparent, dialectical logic that prioritizes physiological safeguards—specifically the protection of the Migrating Motor Complex (MMC)—without sacrificing metabolic efficiency.

🛠 Technical Pillars & Logic
1. The Metabolic Engine (Katch-McArdle Integration)
Unlike standard Mifflin-St Jeor models, this system utilizes the Katch-McArdle formula. By calculating the Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) based on Lean Body Mass (LBM) rather than just total weight, the app provides a highly accurate energy expenditure model for users with specific body composition goals.

2. SIBO Protection Protocol (MMC Guard)
The core architectural constraint is the "Basal Floor Rule." The algorithm prevents the caloric deficit from ever dropping below the user's BMR. This ensures the gut has sufficient energy to maintain the Migrating Motor Complex, preventing the metabolic "stalling" and bacterial stasis common in extreme caloric restriction.

3. Elastic Nutrition Architecture (60/40 Hybrid Loading)
The app implements a dynamic, post-exercise nutritional adjustment logic:

Post-Exertion Recovery: Caloric surpluses generated by exercise are automatically reallocated in a 60/40 ratio.

Glycogen Refill (60%): Prioritizes starches (rice/potatoes) to trigger GLUT4 translocation and glycogen resynthesis.

Caloric Density (40%): Utilizes healthy fats (EVOO) to maintain energy density without exceeding gastrointestinal volume limits (SIBO-safe portions).

4. Training Philosophy: Minimum Effective Dose (MED)
The system tracks activity based on METs (Metabolic Equivalent of Task), focusing on high-density stimuli rather than sheer volume:

Isometrics: Maximum motor unit recruitment with zero joint-shear stress (ideal for chronic shoulder/wrist sensitivity).

HIIT: Targeted metabolic signaling to improve insulin sensitivity and mitochondrial flexibility.

📊 Analyst’s Perspective
As a Functional Analyst, this project represents my commitment to structural thinking and data integrity. Every feature is grounded in physiological "Business Rules":

Zero-Inference Data: No "guessed" calories; everything is based on weight-dependent METs.
